Stripping out the weather

Am I actually using less — or was it just a mild month?

Raw usage rises and falls with the weather, which hides real change. This index compares each month to what my old house would have used in the same weather. Below 1.0 means I beat the old house. The markers show when the new pumps went in and when the 77°/68° thermostat discipline began.

1.0 = exactly what the old pumps + old thermostat habits would have used in that month's weather (fit from — baseline months). Lower is better.
The HVAC lens

My home's energy signature

Every day plotted against its outdoor temperature. The U-shape is the story of the house: heating on the cold left, cooling on the warm right, a low "comfort band" in the middle. Holding 77° in summer and 68° in winter should flatten the steep ends over time — and the new pumps already pulled the warm-weather days down (green vs. grey).

Grey = old pumps (through Apr 10 2026). Green = new variable-speed pool + hot tub pumps (Apr 11 2026 on). As the consistent-setpoint months accumulate, watch the warm-side and cold-side slopes flatten — that's the thermostat discipline showing up.
